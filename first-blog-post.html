7 Skincare Mistakes I Made in My 20s (That I Wish Someone Had Told Me About)

Honest confessions from someone who learned the hard way — so you don't have to.

The Daily Glow June 23, 2026 7 min read

Let me be honest with you — my skin has been through a lot. Not because of genetics or bad luck, but because of me. The choices I made, the things I ignored, and the advice I thought didn't apply to me. If you're in your 20s right now, please read this like it's a letter from your future self.

These are the 7 skincare mistakes I made — some of them for years — and exactly what I do differently now.

Mistake 01

Skipping Sunscreen Indoors 🌥️

I used to think sunscreen was only for the beach. Sunny day outside? Maybe I'd put some on. But staying at home? Absolutely not. I genuinely believed I was safe indoors.

Here's what nobody told me — UV rays come through windows. Every single day I sat near a window, scrolled my phone (blue light is real too), or even sat under harsh indoor lighting, my skin was quietly aging. The dark spots I started noticing in my mid-20s? Hello, indoor sun damage.

✦ Glow Fix: Apply SPF 30 or higher every morning as the last step of your skincare — even on cloudy days, even at home. Make it a habit like brushing your teeth.
Mistake 02

Putting Makeup On Acne Without Prepping My Skin 💄

Acne appeared, I panicked, and I reached for full-coverage foundation. No primer, no moisturizer, no barrier — just makeup straight onto irritated, angry skin. I thought I was hiding the problem. I was actually making it so much worse.

Makeup on unprepped, acne-prone skin clogs pores even more. It sits in the wrong places, breaks down faster, and by the end of the day, your skin is more inflamed than when you started.

✦ Glow Fix: Always cleanse, apply a light non-comedogenic moisturizer, and let your skin breathe for 2 minutes before any makeup. On breakout days, try a tinted SPF instead of heavy foundation.
Mistake 03

Sleeping on a Regular Cotton Pillowcase 😴

This one sounds too small to matter — until you learn what's actually happening to your skin every night. Cotton pillowcases create friction. Every time you move in your sleep, that fabric is tugging at your skin, absorbing your moisturizer, and pressing bacteria right back onto your face.

I spent money on serums and creams and then literally rubbed them all off on my pillowcase by morning. Eight hours of undoing everything I'd just done.

✦ Glow Fix: Switch to a silk or satin pillowcase. It reduces friction, keeps your products on your face where they belong, and is genuinely one of the easiest upgrades you can make for your skin and hair.
Mistake 04

Getting Wax Done on Acne-Prone Skin 😬

I wanted smooth, hair-free skin and walked into the salon without thinking twice. What I walked out with was red, inflamed, bumpy skin that took days to calm down. Waxing on acne-prone skin is one of the most damaging things you can do — it strips the skin's protective barrier, opens up pores, and spreads bacteria across your face.

If you already have active breakouts and you wax over them, you're practically inviting more acne to show up as a plus-one.

✦ Glow Fix: If you have acne-prone skin, opt for threading instead of waxing — it's gentler and doesn't touch the skin the same way. Always tell your esthetician about your skin type before any treatment.
Mistake 05

Popping Pimples with My Bare Hands 🤦‍♀️

We all know we shouldn't. We all do it anyway. I would see a pimple and immediately try to "deal with it." What followed was always the same — redness, a bigger spot, and sometimes a scar that stayed for months.

Your hands carry bacteria. When you pop a pimple, you're pushing that bacteria deeper into the skin and spreading it to the surrounding area. The pimple you "popped" becomes three more pimples within two days.

✦ Glow Fix: Use a hydrocolloid pimple patch instead. It draws out the fluid overnight without you touching your face at all. Game changer — seriously.
Mistake 06

Using Too Many Products at Once 🧴

I went through a phase where I thought more products meant better skin. I had 12 steps, three different acids, two serums, and a face oil — all layered on top of each other every night. My skin was overwhelmed, constantly breaking out, and I couldn't figure out what was helping or hurting.

Skincare isn't about quantity. It's about the right ingredients for your skin, in the right order, consistently.

✦ Glow Fix: Start with just 3 products — a gentle cleanser, a moisturizer, and SPF. Build slowly from there. Give each new product 4 weeks before judging whether it works.
Mistake 07

Never Removing Makeup Before Bed 🌙

Long day, exhausted, just want to sleep. I would tell myself "just this once" and wake up with mascara on my pillow and foundation-clogged pores. Except "just this once" was happening three times a week.

Sleeping in makeup is one of the fastest ways to age your skin. It mixes with oil and pollution from the day, sits in your pores all night, and breaks down your skin's ability to repair itself while you sleep — which is exactly when it's supposed to be doing its best healing work.

✦ Glow Fix: Keep micellar water and cotton pads on your nightstand. On your laziest nights, that's all you need — one swipe and your skin can breathe again. No excuses needed.

Looking back, I don't regret any of it — because every mistake taught me something. My skin today isn't perfect, but it's healthier, calmer, and more understood than it's ever been. And honestly? That feels better than perfect.

Your skin is talking to you. 🌸

It tells you when it's stressed, when it's happy, and when you've been ignoring it for too long. The best thing you can do in your 20s is start listening — even if you've already made a few of these mistakes (we all have).

Small changes, done consistently, will always win over complicated routines done occasionally.
